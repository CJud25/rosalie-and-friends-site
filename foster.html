How Fostering Works

You give the home; the rescue stays behind you. Exact arrangements are worked out with the team when you're matched.

The Rescue Brings

  • Dogs vetted through the rescue's program
  • A volunteer team to guide you as questions come up
  • A backup plan — the dog always has a place with the rescue

You Provide

  • A safe, loving, indoor home
  • Daily care, patience, and routine
  • Rides to vet appointments when you're able
  • Photos and updates that help your foster get noticed

Foster Questions, Answered

Does fostering cost me anything?

Fostering is never meant to be a financial burden. The rescue stays responsible for its dogs — talk with the team about how vet care and supplies are handled for your specific foster.

Can I adopt my foster dog?

Absolutely — we call it a “foster win,” and it's one of our favorite outcomes. Tell the team you'd like to adopt and they'll walk you through the application.

What if it's not a good fit?

Then we rematch, no guilt attached. An honest "this isn't working" helps us learn what the dog needs. The dog always has a place with the rescue.

What if I travel or go on vacation?

Reach out to the team early and they'll help sort out coverage — the dog always has a place with the rescue.

I've never had a senior or special-needs dog. Can I still fospice foster?

Yes. We walk you through everything — medications, comfort care, what to watch for. Willingness matters more than experience.
